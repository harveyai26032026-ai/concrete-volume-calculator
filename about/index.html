About Concrete Calculator

Concrete Calculator is a free, client‑side web tool that instantly estimates concrete volume, bag counts, mix ratios, and saw‑cut spacing. It runs entirely in your browser—no sign‑up, no paywall, and no data is sent to our servers.

Who We Are

Concrete Calculator was built by the Nexus Web Tools team, an Australian technology group with over a decade of experience in software development and construction estimation. After years working on enterprise project-management systems for the construction and engineering sectors, Nexus Web Tools saw a gap: most online concrete calculators were either too simplistic, inaccurate, or buried in intrusive ads. This tool was built to provide accurate, standards‑aligned results in a clean, fast interface.

Our Expertise

  • Construction estimation: Volume calculations support rectangular slabs, cylindrical columns, and graded/uneven ground — covering the most common residential and commercial pour scenarios.
  • Standards alignment: Mix‑ratio guidance references Australian Standard AS 3600 (Concrete Structures) as a baseline, with adjustable ratios for custom jobs.
  • Practical experience: Bag counts are calculated using industry-standard yield figures (e.g., 20 kg bags yield ~0.01 m³) and account for waste factors — something most free tools overlook.
  • Software engineering: The tool is built with vanilla JavaScript, runs entirely client‑side, and is deployed via GitHub Pages for transparency and reliability.

What the Tool Does

The calculator supports both metric and imperial units, offers advanced options such as graded‑land calculations for uneven ground, and provides industry‑standard mix‑ratio guidance. It also includes a saw‑cut spacing calculator based on joint spacing rules of thumb for concrete slabs.

Key features include:

  • Volume estimation for slabs, columns, footings, and irregular shapes
  • Bag count output for 20 kg and 40 kg bags, with waste factor adjustment
  • Mix ratios from 1:2:4 (N20) through to 1:1:2 (N40), aligned with AS 3600
  • Saw‑cut spacing based on slab thickness and joint design rules
  • Graded land support for sloped or uneven pour surfaces
